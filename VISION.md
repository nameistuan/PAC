# Juggle — Motivation & Vision

## The Problem

People juggling multiple areas of their life — classes, work, side projects, personal commitments — don't have a single app that ties it all together. They cobble together a calendar for scheduling, a task manager for to-dos, and a notes app for everything else. The result is constant context-switching, scattered information, and things falling through the cracks.

Google Calendar is where most people start, and it works well enough as a time grid. But it was never designed to be a daily command center. The more you try to use it as one — organizing by life context, managing tasks alongside events, capturing quick thoughts — the more friction you hit. This problem is especially acute for college students, who every semester need to spin up new organizational structures for each class, track assignments with real depth, and keep their academic and personal lives from blurring together. But the underlying frustrations are universal: anyone managing multiple contexts in their life runs into the same walls.

## Pain Points with Traditional Calendars

### 1. Organizing by Life Context is Painfully Manual
Whether it's classes each semester, client projects, or different areas of personal life, people want to visually separate and color-code their different contexts. Something that should take seconds takes minutes of navigating menus. There's no concept of a "project" or "context" as a first-class organizational unit.

### 2. Editing Events Has Too Much Friction
Google Calendar forces me through a full edit page just to change one field. For an app I'm living in all day, this friction adds up. We need fast, inline editing: click, change, done.

### 3. Tasks and Events Are Disconnected
This is the biggest pain point. Tasks cannot be easily assigned to a specific calendar or context. The workaround many people end up using is turning tasks into events just so they can attach them to the right calendar. That's a hack, not a workflow. Events and tasks should be equals, both living under the same organizational structure.

### 4. Tasks Lack Depth
Even when I do use calendar tasks, they're just checkboxes with a title and a due date. Real task management needs more: statuses (to-do, in progress, done), tags, checklists, descriptions, and priority levels. 

### 5. No Space for Unstructured Thought
A day isn't just events and tasks. There are thoughts, quick reminders, things to jot down, reflections, notes from a meeting or lecture. People end up scattering notes across different apps, losing the connection between what they wrote and when they wrote it.

## The Core Vision

**Juggle** is a single "home base" app — something that's open all day and combines scheduling, task management, and notes in one unified experience. A personal command center for anyone managing multiple areas of their life. 

- **Projects & Contexts as a Core Concept.** Each area of life is a first-class organizational unit — easy to create, color-coded, and visually distinct. Events and tasks both belong to these contexts.
- **A Calendar That Shows Everything.** Events and tasks appear together.
- **A Task Board with Real Depth.** Every context has statuses, tags, subtasks, descriptions, and due dates.
- **Fast, Fluid Interaction Everywhere.** Inline and snappy. No full-page modals for simple changes.

## The Paradigm Shift: The "Unified Canvas"

To achieve this without creating "two separate apps in a trench coat" (a calendar app and a task board behind a toggle switch), Juggle introduces the **Unified Canvas**. 

Instead of switching modes, planning happens on one continuous horizontal workspace:
* **A Single Workspace:** The Kanban task board (Backlog, To Do, In Progress) and chronological calendar (Today, Tomorrow, etc.) exist on the same physical plane. 
* **Fluid Drag-and-Drop Synthesis:** You simply pan left to see your high-level project tasks, and drag a task from the "To Do" column directly right onto "Tuesday at 2PM". The task retains its depth but is now beautifully time-boxed on the grid.
* **Global Context Filtering:** Clicking a Project on the sidebar instantly filters *both* the task columns and the calendar columns simultaneously, making it a true command center for that project.

## The Day Column Overhaul

A day is no longer just a time grid. Every day on the calendar represents a multi-purpose command center divided into three seamless vertical zones:
1. **The Journal (Top):** An unstructured rich-text space for thoughts, reflections, and quick reminders. Woven directly into the planner.
2. **The Checklist (Middle):** Tasks strictly due today, giving you a quick hit-list of what needs to get done without a rigid time slot.
3. **The Time Grid (Bottom):** The chronological schedule showing appointments and your time-boxed tasks.

## Who This Is For

For anyone managing multiple areas of their life who's tired of stitching together separate tools. 

**Starting with students.** College students are the launch audience and the wedge market. Their pain is intense and recurring: every semester brings a new set of classes and deadlines that need to be organized from scratch. 

**Built for everyone.** The product uses universal language — projects, tasks, events, notes. A freelancer, a creator, a professional juggling work projects and side hustles would all use Juggle the same way a student uses it with three classes.

## What Success Looks Like

Someone opens Juggle at the start of their day. They see their projects, their tasks, their schedule, and a space to write. They can add a new task to a project in two clicks. They can drag it to a time slot to block out work time. They can check off subtasks as they make progress. They can jot down a thought without leaving the page. Everything is connected, everything is fast, and nothing is buried in a settings menu.
